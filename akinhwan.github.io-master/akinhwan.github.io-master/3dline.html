3D LINES

  • Author: Andrew I. Kim
  • Website: akinhwan.com
  • Date: July 1, 2016
  • Tags: Generative Art, Creative Code

I was imagining a sort of data visualization that wasn't so two-dimensional. At first this sketch was going to be a 3D Line graph of sorts that plotted the cardinal directions of my every step throughout any given day. A complete 360 degrees of motion seemed necessary to mirror the north, northeast, east, south east, south, south west, west, and north west directions. Whether I thought of it as cardinal directions, radians, or degrees, I knew I wanted to see some sort of line graph traversing the z-axis as the x and y coordinates were determined by the heading of my daily walk.

It was an attempt to quantify myself, and something that every bi-pedal human being shares in common...Walking. I attempted to use a combination of motion sensors in my android phone to procure the raw data that would inform my sketch. But as I began to prototype the Processing sketch on its own, it quickly became the main use of my time and much more exciting since there was visual feedback and this was what the end result would have looked like anyways. I know a data-driven visualization would have been that much more real, informative, and narrative. Perhaps in the future I will learn how to incorporate APIs and data wrangle better to give my sketches life beyond the token random functions.
