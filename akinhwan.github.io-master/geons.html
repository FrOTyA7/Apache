A. I. K.

GEONS

  • Author: Andrew Inhwan Kim
  • Medium: Sketch, Pen & Paper, Wacom Tablet
  • Date: August 22 2015
  • Tags: Design, Iconography

These shapes were created with the re-combination of parts of your everyday triangles, circles, squares, and hexagons. The variety of icons shown are created through rotation, layering, re-sizing, cropping, subtracting, stroking, and judicious placement. For the most part, I had no initial idea what the final shape would result in, they arose purely from the experimentation. They are shown in black and white for simplicity sake, knowing that color can be added at any time for future applications of these forms.

Since conception, these shapes have gone on to become Swatches for Pattern-work, Logos for clients, Badges for Web User Gamification, and Inspiration for animations.
